Lore

A Fighter is no mere blade wielder. Instead, it describes a person who's devoted its life to mastery of many fighting styles. A fighter is adept with shields and every form of armor. Beyond that basic degree of familiarity, each fighter specializes in a certain style of combat. Some concentrate on archery, some on fighting with two weapons at once, and some on augmenting their martial skills with magic. This combination of broad general ability and extensive specialization makes fighters superior combatants on battlefields and in dungeons alike.

Class Features

Hit Points. Your hit die is d10, so your starting hp is 10 + CON. For each new level, gain 1d10 + CON.

Proficiencies. All armors and weapons. Saving throws: Strength, Constitution. Skills: choose one from Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, and Survival.

Equipment. In addition to equipment granted by your background: chainmail or leather and longbow with 20 arrows; a martial weapon and shield or two martial weapons; a dungeoneer's pack or an explorer's pack.

Fighting Style

You adopt a particular style of combat as your specialty. Provided you get a second choice you cannot choose the same style again.

Archery. Gain +2 to attack rolls made with ranged weapons.

Defense. While you are wearing armor, gain +1 to AC.

Dueling. When you are wielding a weapon in one hand and the other is free, gain +2 to damage with that weapon.

Great Weapon Fighting. When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a two-handed or versatile weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2.

Protection. When a creature attacks an ally next to you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll, provided you hold a shield.

Two Weapon Fighting. When fighting with two weapons, you can add your damage modifier to the second attack.

Second Wind

You have a deep well of stamina that you can draw on to protect yourself from harm. Once per short or long rest, you can use a bonus action to regain hit points equal to 1d10 + your fighter level.

Action Surge

You can push yourself beyond you limits for a brief moment, and on a turn you can take two actions. You can only do this once (twice at level 17) before a short or long rest.

Ability Score Improvement

Increase one ability score by 2 points, or two ability scores by 1. You cannot, however, increase an attribute over 20. This limitation is removed, however, once you achieve level 20.

Extra Attack

When taking the Attack action, you can make an extra attack (two more at level 11, three more at level 20).

Indomitable

You can reroll a saving throw that you fail. If you do so, you must use the new roll, and you can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest.

Opportunist

Your attacks of opportunity no longer consume your reaction. Furthermore, situations that otherwise negated an attack of opportunity now merely impose disadvantage (such as forced movement).

Martial Archetype

Champion

The archetypal Champion focuses on the development of raw physical power honed to deadly perfection. Those who model themselves on this archetype combine rigorous training with physical excellence to deal devastating blows.

Improved Critical. Gained at level 3. Your weapon attacks score a critical on a roll of 19-20.

Get Down! Gained at level 7. As long as you aren't incapacitated, restrained, grappled or paralyzed, friendly creatures adjacent to you gain +2 to Dexterity saving throws.

Additional Fighting Style. Gained at level 10. Choose a second option from the fighting style list.

Superior Critical. Gained at level 15. Your weapon attacks score a critical on a roll of 18-20.

Survivor. Gained at level 18. You attain the pinnacle of resilience in battle. At the start of each of your turns, you regain 5 + CON hp if you have no more than half of your hit points left. You don't gain this benefit if you have 0 hp.

Honed Reflexes. Gained at level 22. After constant training and with plenty of battle experience, you have reached the peak of physical capacity. Gain proficiency in Dexterity saving throws.


Battle Master

Those who emulate the archetypal Battle Master employ martial techniques passed down through generations. To a Battle Master, combat is an academic field, sometimes including subjects beyond battle such as weaponsmithing and calligraphy. Not every fighter absorbs the lessons of history, theory, and artistry that are reflected in the Battle Master archetype, but those who do are well-rounded fighters of great skill and knowledge.

Combat Superiority. Gained at level 3. You have four superiority dice, which are d8s. A superiority die is expended when you use it. You regain all of your expended superiority dice when you finish a short or long rest.

You also learn three maneuvers (detailed below under Combat Maneuvers), which can be used during an attack (but only one per attack). When learning new maneuvers, you can replace one you already know with a different maneuver.

Know Your Enemy. Gained at level 7. If you spend at least 1 minute observing or interacting with another creature outside combat, you can learn certain information about its capabilities compared to your own. The DM tells you if the creature is your equal, superior, or inferior in regard to two of the following characteristics of your choice: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, AC, current hp, total levels, fighter levels.

Gain another superiority die.

Improved Combat Superiority. Gained at level 10. Gain another 2 maneuvers. Your superiority dice become d10s.

Relentless. Gained at level 15. Gain another 2 maneuvers. When you roll initiative and have no superiority dice remaining, you regain 1 superiority die.

Combat Supremacy. Gained at level 18. Your superiority dice become d12s and you gain one more superiority die.

Master Tactician. Gained at level 22. You develop an almost supernatural instinct for battle, and you can quickly order your team mates in position, even if they are surprised. When initiative is rolled, your allies can move up to 2 squares to better position themselves for the upcoming battle. This effect doesn't stack, and it doesn't trigger if you are surprised.


Combat Maneuvers

Commander's Strike. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can forgo one of your attacks and use a bonus action to direct one of your companions to strike. When you do so, choose an ally who can see or hear you and expend one superiority die. That creature can immediately use its reaction to make one weapon attack, adding the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Disarming Attack. When attempting to disarm a creature, you can expend one superiority die to gain advantage on this check.

Distracting Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to distract the creature, giving your allies an opening, You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll. The next attack roll against the target by an attacker other than you has advantage if the attack is made before the start of your next turn.

Evasive Footwork. When you move, you can expend one superiority die, rolling the die and adding the number rolled to your AC until you stop moving.

Feinting Attack. You can expend one superiority die and use a bonus action on your turn to feint, choosing one creature next to you as your target. You have advantage on your next attack roll against that creature. If that attack hits, add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Goading Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to goad the target into attacking you. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Wisdom save or the target has disadvantage on all attack rolls against targets other than you until the end of your next turn.

Lunging Attack. When you make a melee weapon attack on your turn, you can expend one superiority die to increase your reach for that attack by 1 square. If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Maneuvering Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to maneuver one of your comrades into a more advantageous position. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and you choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you. That creature can use its reaction to move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks from the target of your attack.

Menacing Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to frighten the target. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Wisdom save or be frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Parry. When another creature damages you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to reduce the damage by the number you roll on your superiority die + DEX.

Precision Attack. When you make a weapon attack roll against a creature, you can expend one superiority die to add it to the roll. You can use this maneuver before or after making the attack roll, but before any effects of the attack are applied.

Pushing Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to drive the target back. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and if the target is Large or smaller, it must make a Strength save or be pushed up to 3 squares away from you.

Rally. On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to bolster the resolve of one of your companions. When you do so, choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you. That creature gains temporary hp equal to the superiority die roll + your Charisma modifier.

Riposte. When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to make a melee weapon attack against that creature, If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Sweeping Attack. When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to damage another creature with the same attack, Choose another creature adjacent to the original target and within your reach. If the original attack roll would hit the second creature, it takes damage equal to the number you roll on your superiority die. The damage is of the same type dealt by the original attack.

Trip Attack. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to knock the target down. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and if the target is Large or smaller, it must make a Strength savior be knocked prone.


Arcane Knight

The archetypal Arcane Knight combines the martial mastery common to all fighters with a careful study of magic. Arcane Knights use magical techniques similar to those practiced by wizards. They focus their study on two of the eight schools of magic: abjuration and protomancy. Abjuration spells grant an Arcane Knight additional protection in battle, while protomancy spells deal damage to many foes at once, extending the fighter's reach in combat. These knights learn a comparatively small number of spells, committing them to memory instead of keeping them in a spellbook.

Spellcasting. Gained at level 3. Gain proficiency in Arcana. Learn two cantrips and a 1st level spell of your choice from the wizard spell list. You also learn two 1st level abjuration or protomancy spells from the wizard spell list.

The Spells Known column of the Arcane Knight Spellcasting table shows when you learn more wizard spells. Each of these spells must be an abjuration or protomancy spell and must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

The spells you learn at 8th, 14th, and 20th level can come from any school of magic. Whenever you gain a level in this c1ass, you can replace one of the wizard spells you know with another spell of your choice from the wizard spell list (abjuration or protomancy, unless you're replacing the spell you gained at 8th, 14th, or 20th level).

Weapon Bond. Gained at level 3. You can bond with up to two weapons through a ritual lasting one hour. You cannot be disarmed of a bonded weapon unless you're incapacitated, and you can summon it to your hand instantly, so long as it is on the same plane of existence.

War Magic. Gained at level 7. When you use your action to cast a cantrip, you can use your bonus action to make a weapon attack.

Arcane Strike. Gained at level 10. You learn how to make your weapon strikes undercut a creature's resistance to your spells. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, that creature has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes against a spell you cast before the end of your next turn.

Learn an additional cantrip from the wizard spell list.

Arcane Charge. Gained at level 15. You gain the ability to teleport up to 6 squares to an unoccupied space you can see when you use your Action Surge. You can teleport before or after the additional action.

Improved War Magic. Gained at level 18. When you use your action to cast a spell, you can use your bonus action to make a weapon attack.

Sundering Arcana. Gained at level 22. You have gained a deeper grasp of arcana, and now you can briefly target the hidden ley lines of a foe, thus weakening their magical defenses. Once per short rest, a successful weapon attack can remove an enemy's resistance to a single damage type, or turn immunity into resistance, until the end of your next turn.
