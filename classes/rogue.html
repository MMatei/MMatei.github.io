Lore

When people hear rogue, they immediately think of thieves, bandits, smugglers, or other types of outlaws. First, not every smalltime crook is a real Rogue. Secondly, being a Rogue does not mean one is selfish, and not even that one lives outside the law. Rogues are merely uniquely talented individuals who prefer working in and fighting from the shadows.

Rogues devote as much effort to mastering the use of a variety of skills as they do to perfecting their combat abilities, giving them a broad expertise that few other characters can match. Many rogues focus on stealth and deception, while others refine the skills that help them in a dungeon environment, such as climbing, finding and disarming traps, and opening locks.

When it comes to combat, rogues prioritize cunning over brute strength. A rogue would rather make one precise strike, placing it exactly where the attack will hurt the target most, than wear an opponent down with a barrage of attacks. Rogues have an almost supernatural knack for avoiding danger, and a few learn magical tricks to supplement their other abilities.

As adventurers, rogues fall on both sides of the law. Some are hardened criminals who decide to seek their fortune in treasure hoards, while others take up a life of adventure to escape from the law. Some have learned and perfected their skills with the explicit purpose of infiltrating ancient ruins and hidden crypts in search of treasure.

Class Features

Hit Points. Your hit die is d8, so your starting hp is 8 + Constitution. For each new level, gain 1d8 + Constitution.

Proficiencies. Light armor, simple weapons, crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords, Reflex and Warding. Skills: choose three from Acrobatics, Athletics, Crafting (Traps), Deception, Escape Artist, Insight, Intimidation, Linguistics, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth.

Equipment. In addition to equipment granted by your background: rapier or shortsword; shortbow with 20 arrows or light crossbow with 20 bolts; a burglar's pack or a dungeoneer's pack or an explorer's pack; leather armor, two daggers and thieves' tools.

Expertise

At 1st level, choose two of your skill proficiencies, or one of your skill proficiencies and your proficiency with thieves' tools. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses either of the chosen proficiencies.

At 6th level, you can choose two more of your proficiencies (in skills or with thieves' tools) to gain this benefit.

Sneak Attack

You know how to strike subtly and exploit a foe's distraction. Once per turn, you can deal extra damage (see class progression table) to one creature you hit with an attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The attack must use a finesse or a ranged weapon.

Thieves' Cant

During your rogue training you learned thieves' cant, a secret mix of dialect, jargon, and code that allows you to hide messages in seemingly normal conversation. Only another creature that knows thieves' cant understands such messages. It takes four times longer to convey such a message than it does to speak the same idea plainly.

In addition, you understand a set of secret symbols that thieves use to mark areas as hideouts, dangerous, note the patrol patterns of guards and other various tidbits that might be of interest to fellow law-breakers.

Cunning Action

Your quick thinking and agility allow you to move and act quickly. You can use your bonus action to take the Dash, Disengage, or Hide action.

Ability Score Improvement

Increase any one ability score under +5 by +1 or choose a feat. Once you achieve level 20, ability scores over +5.

Uncanny Dodge

When an attacker that you can see hits you with an attack, you can use your reaction to halve the attack's damage against you.

Evasion

You can nimbly dodge out of the way of damaging area effects, such as a red dragon's fiery breath or an ice storm spell. When you are subjected to an effect that attacks your Dexterity whose failure would still deal half damage, then you take half damage if it succeeeds, and no damage if it fails.

Reliable Talent

You have refined your chosen skills until they approach perfection. Whenever you make an ability check that lets you add your proficiency bonus, you can treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

Blindsense

If you're able to hear, you're aware of the location of any creature within 2 squares of you.

Slippery Mind

Your cunning now enables you to better defend yourself against mental assaults. You gain proficiency in Intuition defense.

Elusive

You are so evasive that attackers rarely gain the upper hand against you. No attack roll has advantage against you while you aren't incapacitated.

Stroke of Genius

Your skills are so sharpened, that when success matters most, you'll inevitably obtain it. If your attack misses a target within range, you can turn the miss into a hit. Alternatively, if you fail an ability check, you can treat the d20 roll as a 20.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

Combat Ready

You're like a coiled spring, ready to pounce on your enemies. You roll initiative with advantage, and you have advantage on attacks against any creature that has yet to take a turn in combat.


Roguish Archetypes

Thief

You hone your skills in the larcenous arts. Burglars, bandits, cutpurses, and other criminals typically follow this archetype, but so do rogues who prefer to think of themselves as professional treasure seekers, explorers, delvers, and investigators. In addition to improving your agility and stealth, you learn skills useful for delving into ancient ruins, reading unfamiliar languages, and using magic items you normally couldn't employ.

Fast Hands. Gained at level 3. You can use the bonus action granted by your Cunning Action to make a Sleight of Hand check, use your thieves' tools to disarm a trap or open a lock, or take the Use Object action.

Second Story Work. Gained at level 3. You are a master climber; climbing no longer costs you extra movement. In addition, when you make a running jump, the distance you can cover doubles.

Supreme Sneak. Gained at level 9. You gain advantage on Stealth checks provided you move no more than half your speed on the current turn.

Use Magic Device. Gained at level 13. You have learned enough about the workings of magic that you can improvise the use of items even when they are not intended for you. You ignore all class, race, and level requirements on the use of magic items.

Thief's Reflexes. Gained at level 17. You have become adept at laying ambushes and quickly escaping danger. You can take two turns during the first round of any combat. You take your first turn at your normal initiative and your second turn at your initiative - 10. You can't use this feature when surprised.

Fast Talker. Gained at level 22. Gain +4 on any Deception, Intimidation, Performance or Persuasion roll.


Assassin

You focus your training on the grim art of death. Those who adhere to this archetype are diverse: hired killers, spies, bounty hunters, and even specially anointed priests trained to exterminate the enemies of their deity. Stealth, poison, and disguise help you eliminate your foes with deadly efficiency.

Assassin Proficiencies. Gained at level 3. You gain extra proficiencies with the disguise kit and the poisoner's kit.

Assassinate. Gained at level 3. You are at your deadliest when you get the drop on your enemies. You have advantage on attack rolls against any creature that hasn't taken a turn in the combat yet. In addition, any hit you score against a creature that is surprised is a critical hit.

Infiltration Expertise. Gained at level 9. You can unfailingly create false identities for yourself. You must spend seven days and 25 gp to establish the history, profession, and affiliations for an identity. You can't establish an identity that belongs to someone else. For example, you might acquire appropriate clothing, letters of introduction, and official-looking certification to establish yourself as a member of a trading house from a remote city so you can insinuate yourself into the company of other wealthy merchants.

If you adopt the new identity as a disguise, other creatures believe you to be that person until given an obvious reason not to.

Impostor. Gained at level 13. You gain the ability to unerringly mimic another person's speech, writing, and behavior. You must spend at least three hours studying these three components of the person's behavior, listening to speech, examining handwriting, and observing mannerisms. Your ruse is indiscernible to the casual observer. If a wary creature suspects something is amiss, you have advantage on any Deception check you make to avoid detection.

Death Strike. Gained at level 18. You become a master of instant death. When you attack and hit a creature that is surprised, roll attack vs CON. On hit, double the damage of your attack against the creature.

Killing Blow. Gained at level 22. You have learned how to exploit the vulnerabilities of the foes you face. When you land a critical strike, all damage die are set to their maximum value. Furthermore, you land a critical strike on a roll of 19-20.


Arcane Trickster

A few very patient rogues choose to enhance their fine-honed skills of stealth and agility with magic, learning tricks of enchantment and illusion. These rogues include high-end burglars and killers, as well as those who venture into dangerous environments.

Arcane Tricksters know only limited magic compared to a wizard, but even this is an awesome effort, for they achieve in months what most wizards achieve in years of (admitedly more general) study. A spellbook in which they write their learned spells is required, for they need to refresh their memory at every long rest from it. Unlike wizards, however, tricksters have all their spells prepared at all times, so long as they have perused their spellbook the night prior.

Cantrips. Learn three cantrips: mage hand and two cantrips of your choice from the wizard spell list. You learn an additional cantrip at level 10.

Spellcasting. Gain proficiency in Arcana; you cannot gain expertise in Arcana. Learn three 1st level wizard spells of your choice, two of which you must choose from the noumancy or umbramancy spells on the wizard spell list. The Spells Known column of the Arcane Trickster Spellcasting table shows when you learn more wizard spells. Each of these spells must be a noumancy or umbramancy spell and must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

The spells you learn at 8th, 14th, and 20th level can come from any school of magic. Whenever you gain a level in this c1ass, you can replace one of the wizard spells you know with another spell of your choice from the wizard spell list (noumancy or umbramancy, unless you're replacing the spell you gained at 8th, 14th, or 20th level).

Your spellbook will always contain merely the spells you know, and further inscriptions are just gibberish to you. You cannot learn any new spells from the writings of others or any other source except Spell Thief - your arcane education is very limited and based more on rote memorisation than understanding.

Legerdemain. Gained at level 3. When you cast mage hand, the hand is invisible and can perform the following additional tasks: you can retrieve or stow an object into a container held by another creature; you can use thieves' tools to disarm traps or pick locks from a distance.

You can hide these tasks from a creature with a Sleight of Hand roll vs Perception. Moreover, you can use the bonus action provided by Cunning Action to control the hand.

Magical Ambush. Gained at level 9. If you are hidden from a creature when you cast a spell on it, you have advantage on rolls made as part of that spell.

Versatile Trickster. Gained at level 13. You gain the ability to distract targets with your mage hand. As a bonus action on your turn, you can designate a creature within a square of the spectral hand created by the spell. Doing so gives you advantage on attack rolls vs AC against that creature until the end of the turn.

Spell Thief. Gained at level 17. You gain the ability to magically steal the knowledge of how to cast a spell from another spellcaster. Immediately after a creature casts a spell that targets you or includes you in its area of effect, you can use your reaction to roll spellpower vs 8 + spellpower. On hit, you negate the spell's effect against you, and you steal the knowledge of the spell if it is at least 1st level and of a level you can cast (it doesn't need to be a wizard spell).

For the next 8 hours, you know the spell and can cast it using your spell slots. The creature can't cast that spell until the 8 hours have passed. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

Surprise! Gained at level 22. You have gained mastery over short range teleportation. You can now use your bonus action to switch places with with any creature within 6 squares of you.
