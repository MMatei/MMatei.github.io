Tigran

Lore

Created during the heyday of the Mydasian Empire as novelties for an increasingly decadent population, the Tigrans spent much of their history as slaves. Only in the chaos of the Empire's collapse did some of them manage to achieve freedom, living as nomadic tribes as far from civilization as they can.

Tigrans are very graceful creatures, with beautiful tiger-like faces and brightly colored eyes. Their fur can have any of a wide variety of luxuriant colors: white, blonde, scarlet, brown or even black. Tigrans are very proud of their appearance and take great effort to maintain it, often to the point of vanity. They even tend to choose mates of the same fur color, so that their children inherit monochrome furs. Mixed marriages don't always produce children with shaggy fur, but when they do, those children are often an object of undue ridicule.

Like most felines, Tigrans are also endowed with surprising agility and an enhanced climbing ability, which they employ for maximum effect as they wander the lands. They like to roam around harsh terrain with steep rocks and plentiful forests, where their enemies would have a harder time at catching up with them. At the same time, though, tigrans have a preference for warmer climates, and quite a significant number has migrated to the jungles of Trax.

Tigrans are absolutely fascinated by jewelry and they prize it extremely highly, often adorning themselves with as many as they can afford. Tigran fashion in fact, is highly oriented towards beauty over practicality, especially for those lucky enough to live in cities. But even the nomadic Tigrans enjoy dressing richly when not on a hunt, and they always build a semi-permanent village where they periodically meet with other tribes to trade, celebrate and mingle.

Tigrans are curious, but also capricious. They may move mountains in pursuit of something that strikes their fancy, but as soon as they acquire it, the object loses all value and may even end up carelessly thrown away. The thrill of the hunt captivates a Tigran above all else.

Tigrans acquire vices more easily than most and are liable to pursue them with abandon. They crave pleasure and will do their best to acquire and prolong it.


Traits

Ability Score Increase. +1 Dexterity.

Age. Tigrans have the same lifespan as humans.

Alignment. Tigrans tend to be somewhat chaotic and selfish, but aren't necessarily downright evil.

Size. Tigrans have about the same height as humans, at 1.6 to 1.8 meters tall. They are somewhat more slender and weigh between 80-100 kg. Their size is Medium.

Speed. Your base walking speed is 7 squares. You also have a climbing speed of 4 squares.

Darkvision. You have feline-like senses, boasting superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 12 squares (18m) of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Feline Agility. Your reflexes and agility allow you to move with a burst of speed. When you move on your turn in combat, you can double your speed until the end of the turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.

Master Climber. You have advantage on checks related to climbing.

Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common and one other language of your choice.
